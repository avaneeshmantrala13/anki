# Data provenance & limitations

**Read this before trusting any number produced by `brainlift_eval/`.**

BrainLift ships with an eval/proof harness, an ablation study, and a benchmark.
Several of the headline numbers are produced from **SIMULATED or fixture data**,
not from real students or the live AI model. This document states plainly, in one
place, *which* numbers are simulated, *how* each simulator generates its data, how
train/test separation is guaranteed, and where the results may not generalize.

> **One-line caveat that applies to everything below:** these are results on
> *synthetic* data generated by deterministic models we wrote. They are evidence
> that the system behaves as designed **under the modeled assumptions**; they are
> **NOT** evidence from real students and **may not hold in the field**. The
> app has never been run against a live student cohort.

---

## Quick map: where does each number come from?

| Number / artifact | Source of data | Real, simulated, or fixture? |
|---|---|---|
| Fatigue model held-out **acc / AUC / log-loss** (`fatigue_model_eval.py`) | `fatigue_sim.py` seeded sessions | **SIMULATED** (train *and* test) |
| Fatigue model **weights** (`train_fatigue_model.py` → shipped in `anki.brainlift.fatigue`) | `fatigue_sim.py` seeded sessions | **SIMULATED** training data |
| Analog-generation evals: held-out, gold-set, baseline, leakage, prompt-injection (`run_eval.py`, `gold_eval.py`, `baseline.py`, `leakage_check.py`, `prompt_injection_check.py`) | `DeterministicAnalogClient` fixture generator (unless `--live`) | **FIXTURE** (deterministic; not the live model) |
| **Paraphrase gap** (`paraphrase_gap.py`) | Seeded memory-strength + transfer simulation | **SIMULATED** |
| **3-build ablation** (`ablation.py`) | Seeded synthetic learner cohort | **SIMULATED** |
| **50k benchmark** (`bench.py`) | Programmatically generated card deck | **SYNTHETIC deck** (latencies are real, on your machine) |
| **Crash test** (`crash_test.py`) | Programmatically generated deck | **SYNTHETIC deck** (durability result is real) |
| Bundled Exam P study content (SOA sample Q&A) | Society of Actuaries published PDFs | **REAL** (see README attribution) |

The only production numbers derived from **real** external data are the FSRS
scheduling (Anki's own algorithm) and the bundled SOA Exam P questions. Every
*evaluation* number about the two AI features is simulated or fixture-based.

## 1. Fatigue model data — `fatigue_sim.py` (SIMULATED train AND test)

**What it is.** There is no live student response data. `fatigue_sim.py`
generates a labeled dataset of *simulated* study sessions. Each session is a
sequence of (response-time, correct?) answers folded through the **shipped**
feature pipeline (`anki.brainlift.fatigue.update_state` /
`model_feature_vector`), so the training features are byte-identical to what the
desktop/mobile engines compute at inference time.

**How it generates data.** Two session profiles, `FRESH` and `DRAINED`, draw
per-answer signals from a seeded RNG with effect sizes calibrated to three
peer-reviewed sustained-attention papers (the model's *named source*):

- Fortenbaugh et al. 2015 (*Psychological Science*) — vigilance decrement, RT
  slowing and rising RT variability with time-on-task.
- Hanzal et al. 2024 (*PLOS ONE*) — accuracy decrement coupled to state fatigue.
- Hassanzadeh-Behbaha et al. 2018 (*Frontiers in Psychology*) — progressive RT
  slowing across successive blocks.

`DRAINED` sessions show progressive slowdown, higher RT variance and an accuracy
drop; `FRESH` sessions stay fast/accurate. The ranges deliberately **overlap** so
the classification task is non-trivial (a perfect score would signal an
unrealistically clean simulation).

**Train/test separation (no leakage).** Train and test are generated from
**disjoint RNG seeds** and are assigned **disjoint session-id namespaces**:

- `TRAIN_SEED = 12345`, `TRAIN_N = 1600`
- `TEST_SEED  = 98765`, `TEST_N  = 600`

`fatigue_model_eval.py` asserts the identity-based guarantee: every training
session id is `("train", 12345, i)` and every test id is `("test", 98765, j)`, so
the two namespaces are provably disjoint (0 shared ids). It additionally reports —
as an *informational* number, not the pass criterion — how many held-out feature
vectors happen to coincide numerically with a training one (also 0).

**Limitation.** The model has never seen a real student. Per-user online
adaptation on real response streams is explicit future work. The 0.90/0.97
acc/AUC describe how well the model separates *our simulated* fresh vs drained
sessions, and would need re-validation on live data.

## 2. Analog-generation evals — deterministic FIXTURE client

By default the analog-generation evals (`run_eval.py`, `gold_eval.py`,
`baseline.py`, `leakage_check.py`, `prompt_injection_check.py`) do **not** call
OpenAI. They use `DeterministicAnalogClient` — a deterministic, offline fixture
generator that returns the same `GeneratedAnalog` structure the live client would.
This keeps the suite reproducible in CI with no key and no network.

- The numbers in `RESULTS.txt` are the **fixture** numbers, not live-model numbers.
- Passing `--live` (with `OPENAI_API_KEY` set) exercises the real `gpt-4o-mini`
  client through the identical checker; those numbers depend on the live model and
  are not committed.

**Limitation.** Offline fixture behaviour is a lower bound / shape-check, not a
measurement of the production model's quality. Treat committed analog-eval numbers
as "the harness works and the fixture path is clean", not "the live model scores X".

## 3. Paraphrase gap — `paraphrase_gap.py` (SIMULATED)

No live students. For each of 30 gold cards, a fixed-seed (`seed=42`) simulation
assigns a "memory strength" for the memorized original and a transfer factor `< 1`
for a reworded analog, then samples 200 attempts/card. The reported "original
recall vs analog accuracy" gap is therefore a property of that simulation. Swap in
real per-user (front-recall vs analog-correct) data for live numbers; the gap
computation is unchanged.

## 4. 3-build ablation — `ablation.py` (SIMULATED)

A mechanistic simulation of a seeded synthetic learner cohort (`MASTER_SEED =
20240501`; held-out test seeds in the disjoint `900000+` namespace). Each learner
has latent per-topic ability, a (mis)calibration bias, and a within-sitting
fatigue vigilance decrement (calibrated to the same fatigue literature as §1).

- **Full app (A)** uses the *shipped* `authority_multiplier` /
  `effective_mastery_gap` formulas (imported from `anki.brainlift.calibration`)
  and a modeled fatigue offload that recovers 50% of the vigilance-decrement loss.
- **Ablation (B)** runs the same scheduling core with both features off.
- **Stock (C)** is plain weight-only / due-order scheduling.

The primary metric (held-out mixed-topic accuracy) is **pre-declared before
results**. Effects are reported as mean ± 95% CI over learners, with paired A−B /
A−C differences and a calibration subgroup decomposition. To avoid a rigged
result, the calibration-authority benefit is intentionally heterogeneous (it only
helps mis-calibrated learners) and the study reports its isolated effect honestly,
even when small/near-null.

**Limitation.** The learners, abilities, mis-calibration and fatigue are all
invented by our generative model. The ablation measures the *modeled* benefit; it
proves the features help **if the modeled mechanisms are real**, and is not
field evidence.

## 5. 50k benchmark & crash test — SYNTHETIC decks (but real measurements)

`bench.py` and `crash_test.py` build decks of programmatically generated cards
(`"Benchmark card N …"`) tagged across the Exam P syllabus. The card *content* is
synthetic filler, but the things being measured are real:

- `bench.py` reports **real wall-clock latencies and peak RSS** on your machine
  (they vary by hardware; treat them as budget checks, not lab figures).
- `crash_test.py` performs **real** unclean process kills mid-review and **real**
  SQLite + Anki integrity checks on reopen; the zero-corruption result is a real
  durability property of the shared Anki storage core.

**Limitation.** Real student decks have different card/tag/media distributions;
latencies on a low-end phone will differ from a desktop. The synthetic deck is a
stress proxy, sized to the stated 50k target.

## Summary of honesty commitments

1. Every simulated/fixture number is labeled as such in its script's output and
   docstring, not just here.
2. Train/test separation for the learned fatigue model is identity-based
   (disjoint session-id namespaces from disjoint seeds), not a fragile
   float-coincidence check.
3. Null / small / negative results (e.g. calibration authority's modest isolated
   ablation effect) are reported, not hidden.
4. We never claim field validation we don't have: **these numbers may not hold on
   real students.**
